Why the career change, you ask?


Last year, I lost my job due to outsourcing. I took this as an opportunity to learn something new. Something where I could learn more and grow in a career.
I have always been interested in tech since I was young. I am an 80's baby, after all! I remember watching my brother play Full Throttle and Doom (to name a couple) for hours on our first MS-DOS computer. There, I fell in love with technology. The fascination only grew as new technology continued to be released. At my former job, I was always the "go-to" person among my co-workers and friends for tech related issues and I knew I had to persue a career in technology.
When I discovered coding and front-end development, I fell in love. I've always been curious about how the internet works. Now knowing how to create actual web pages and applications, I am beyond thrilled and excited to begin a career in development. I love that the learning is limitless. I enjoy the thrill of learning something new and being able to apply it; That I'm doing something with meaning and doing something I love, while being a part of something bigger than myself.
